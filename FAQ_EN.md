### Can I use it in a funded research project?
**Yes**, if:
- The research is non-commercial (even if funded)
- The software is a research tool, not the object of funding
- You properly cite the software in publications

**Example OK**: Study on attention disorders using the tachistoscope for tests  
**Example NOT OK**: Software development project funded to create a commercial product based on this tachistoscope

### Can I publish my research results?
**Yes**, provided you cite the software as indicated in the license.

## üè• CLINICAL USE

### Can I use it with my patients?
**Yes**, if you're a clinical professional (psychologist, psychotherapist, speech therapist, etc.) you can use it during your sessions or assessments.

### Can I bill sessions where I use the software?
**Yes**, you can bill the professional service (consultation, assessment, therapy). The compensation must be for your service, not for software use.

### Can my clinical center use it?
**Yes**, clinical centers, clinics, hospitals can use it as a support tool for clinical activity.

### Can I include it in a validated clinical protocol?
**Yes**, provided the protocol itself isn't commercialized as a product.

### Do I need to inform patients that I use this software?
Follow regulations and your professional obligations. If you must inform patients about tools used, mention the software citing the author.

**No**, you cannot distribute modified versions outside of GitHub. However, you can:
- Contribute improvements through pull requests
- Keep your fork on GitHub with modifications

## üí∞ COMMERCIAL USE

### What exactly does "commercial use" mean?
Commercial use means:
- Selling the software
- Including it in sold products
- Offering it as a paid service (SaaS, apps, subscriptions)
- Using it as a main component of a business

### Can my company use it internally?
**Gray area**. If you use it only internally as a support tool (e.g., private clinical center using it for patients), probably yes. If it becomes part of your sold product/service, no. Contact the author for clarification.

### Can I create a free app that includes it?
**No**, even if free, distributing the software in an app is considered commercial use. Contact the author for a dedicated license.

### Can I use it in a free online service?
**No**, offering the software as a service (even free) requires authorization. Contact the author.

## ü§ù CONTRIBUTIONS

### If I submit a pull request, what happens to my rights?
- You keep copyright of your code
- You're recognized as a contributor
- You grant the author the right to use your contribution in the project
- Your contribution will be under the same license
- **You can mention your contribution in your CV, portfolio, or LinkedIn**

### Can I remove my contribution after submitting it?
No, accepted contributions become a permanent part of the project.

### How can I professionally showcase my contribution?
You can:
- Include it in your CV under "Open Source Projects" or "GitHub Contributions"
- Add it to your portfolio with screenshots and description
- Mention it on LinkedIn in the projects or experience section
- Link the pull request or issue in your description

**Example for CV/Portfolio**:
```
Contribution to Tachistoscope Project (GitHub)
- Implemented feature X [PR link]
- Fixed critical bugs [issue links]
- Improved documentation
```
